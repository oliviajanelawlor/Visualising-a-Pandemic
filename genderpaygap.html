“Women’s equality is simply not a priority for this government”: Feminist organisation concerned after gender pay gap data suspended

A feminist organisation are concerned after the government suspended deadlines for companies gender pay gap data due to the Coronavirus.

Almost half (47%) of companies with 250 employees or more didn’t submit their gender pay gap data during 2019/20 after the government suspended reporting deadlines because of the Coronavirus.

In a statement released in March by Liz Truss, Minister for Women and Equalities, and David Issac, EHRC Chair, said: “We recognise that employers across the country are facing unprecedented uncertainty and pressure at this time.

“Because of this, we feel it is only right to suspend enforcement of the gender pay gap reporting this year.”

In normal circumstances, if employers don’t submit their gender pay gap data, the EHRC (Equality and Human Rights Commission) have the authority to investigate.

This can lead to companies facing the maximum penalty in Crown Court of an unlimited fine after court action.

Due to the suspension of deadlines, there were over 5000 fewer companies that didn’t submit their gender pay gap data compared to the previous year.

What is the gender pay gap?

The gender pay gap is the difference between the average hourly wage between men and women in the workplace. For example, if more women do less well-paid jobs in a company compared to men, the gender pay gap may, and is usually bigger.

In 1970, The Equal Pay Act in the UK was introduced to ban the unequal treatment between men and women in terms of pay and the conditions of the employment.
